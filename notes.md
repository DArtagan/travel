# Zermatt
* Switzerland: excellent train system (website is excellent, the entire thing is treated with the convenience of a commuter system, site even has handy inline maps for transfers and the like). Makers of excellent doors and incredibly well sealed rooms.
* Marsanne 2016: very tasty wine.  This one was known to be "oakey".
* On skiing Zermatt
    Zermatt was infrastructurally incredibly impressive. Vast distinct mountains, every kind of chairlift/gondola/cable-car/funicular/cog-train connecting them (an uphill capacity of 90,000 riders/hour). Houses and restaurants in the middle of ski trails, which you could pop into for a hearty lunch.
The skiing itself though: I've never been less inspired by a mountain. In Zermatt's defense, there are two contributing factors: #1. they hadn't had fresh snow in about two weeks - it was pretty northeast-style icy. And I think that matters more because of how the mountain is structured. Imagine a familiar ski resort map - picture all the main groomer trails, separated by trees. In the alps, timberline is much lower - so in your imagination, erase all the trees. In America, the resort largely takes responsibility for everything within its boundaries - if it's not blocked off, then it's considered skiable. In Europe, only the groomer trails, the piste, are guaranteed. Everything "off-piste" is skier beware: rocks, dirt, ravines, crevasses, avalanches, etc. So because they had no snow, everything off-piste was pretty fraught. So it was long (icy) groomers all-day with lots of traverses.
    Factor #2. I went off coffee when I got to Zermatt. My enthusiasm was probably impacted.
